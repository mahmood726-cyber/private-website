How should the OpenPalp content sit within your website?

The booklet describes a focused palpitations programme. Your current site has two pages: a general cardiology landing page (index.html) and a palpitations pathway page (pathway.html). How should these relate?

A

OpenPalp becomes the entire site

Replace both index.html and pathway.html with a single, polished OpenPalp-focused site. The clinic is the palpitations programme. Drop the general cardiology conditions (blood pressure, breathlessness, murmurs).

Best if: The clinic is pivoting entirely to the OpenPalp model. One clear message, one clear offering.
B

OpenPalp replaces pathway.html; index.html stays as a landing page

Keep the general clinic landing page (index.html) but make OpenPalp the primary feature — hero section links to it prominently. The other conditions remain listed but secondary. pathway.html gets a complete rewrite with all booklet content and diagrams.

Best if: You still want to offer general consultations alongside the structured programme.
C

OpenPalp replaces pathway.html entirely; index.html updated to point to it

pathway.html becomes the comprehensive OpenPalp page with all booklet content, diagrams, evidence, device info, 6-week journey. index.html is simplified to a brief "welcome" that funnels visitors to the OpenPalp pathway as the main offering, but keeps the clinic identity and basic info.

Best if: OpenPalp is the main product but you want a simple branded front door.

How It Works

A stepped pathway from assessment to improvement

The Rapid Rhythm Assessment Pathway combines clinical review, structured monitoring, and a practical improvement programme over approximately six weeks.

Week 0

Clinic appointment

A focused face-to-face assessment. We review your symptoms, triggers, risk factors, and medical history. If the low-risk pathway is appropriate, we discuss next steps and provide your monitoring device.

Weeks 0–2

Kardia ECG monitoring (a small handheld heart monitor)

You use a Kardia device to record 30-second ECGs whenever you experience symptoms, and at regular intervals. Recordings are submitted and reviewed within the pathway.

Weeks 2–4 (if needed)

Extended monitoring

If Kardia monitoring has not captured a diagnostic rhythm during symptoms, selected patients may move to a higher-quality wearable ECG device for continuous or extended recording.

Weeks 4–6 (if clinically appropriate)

Final monitoring phase

If no diagnostic capture has occurred and it remains clinically appropriate, up to two further weeks of monitoring may be offered. No monitoring extends beyond six weeks within this standard pathway.

Weeks 0–6

Six-week improvement programme

Running alongside monitoring, the programme covers breathing, trigger mapping, sleep, graded activity, structured reassurance, and learning to respond calmly to symptoms. Designed to help you feel measurably better.

At any point

Escalation if needed

If monitoring reveals a significant rhythm finding — SVT, atrial fibrillation, significant pauses, or high ectopy burden — you are referred onward to NHS or private cardiology as appropriate.

What if something significant is found?

This pathway is designed for low-risk assessment and reassurance. If monitoring identifies a significant abnormality, we do not manage it in isolation. You will be referred to an appropriate specialist — NHS or private — with documented findings and a clear summary.

Suitability

Is this pathway right for you?

This service is designed for selected low-risk patients. Please review the criteria below carefully.

This pathway is designed for you if

  • You are aged 18 or over
  • You have intermittent palpitations — an awareness of your heartbeat
  • Episodes are brief, lasting seconds to minutes
  • You feel generally well between episodes
  • You have no known significant heart disease
  • Symptoms include missed beats, skipped beats, or occasional rapid episodes that settle on their own
  • Symptoms are associated with caffeine, stress, poor sleep, or anxiety
  • You have not been diagnosed with a high-risk arrhythmia

This pathway is not suitable if

  • You have fainted or nearly fainted
  • You have chest pain that may be cardiac in origin
  • You have severe or unexplained breathlessness
  • Your heart races for more than 20–30 minutes without settling
  • You have known structural heart disease or heart failure
  • You have a known significant arrhythmia requiring management
  • You have had cardiac surgery or complex congenital heart disease
  • Your symptoms are clearly triggered by exertion
  • You have a strong family history of sudden cardiac death
  • You have an abnormal baseline ECG that needs investigation

Borderline — clinical judgement required

  • More frequent or prolonged episodes
  • Mild exertional symptoms without other red flags
  • Unclear history
  • Previous investigations but persistent symptoms

These patients may enter the pathway after clinician review, or be referred onward directly.

If you have chest pain, fainting, severe breathlessness, or feel acutely unwell, do not use this service. Seek urgent medical attention immediately — call 999 or attend A&E.

What if monitoring finds something significant?

Even in low-risk patients, monitoring may occasionally reveal a significant finding such as SVT, atrial fibrillation, significant pauses, or high ectopy burden. If this happens, you are referred onward to NHS or private cardiology as appropriate. Symptoms that remain unexplained after the pathway are also escalated.

The Programme

Six weeks of structured support

Seven practical interventions designed to help you understand your symptoms and feel measurably better. No dense science — just clear, actionable steps.

Breathe

A simple breathing protocol that helps regulate your nervous system. Slow, controlled breathing can reduce the frequency and intensity of palpitations for many people.

Track

Heart rate variability (HRV) feedback helps you understand how your body responds to stress and recovery. You learn to recognise patterns and respond to them.

Map your triggers

Identify what makes your symptoms more likely — caffeine, alcohol, poor sleep, stress, dehydration. Once you know your triggers, you can make informed choices.

Move

Graded activity guidance tailored to your fitness level. Regular gentle activity supports cardiovascular health and is associated with reduced palpitation frequency.

Sleep

Sleep quality has a direct relationship with heart rhythm. We provide a structured sleep optimisation plan covering timing, environment, and habits.

Reduce triggers

Practical, non-judgemental guidance on reducing lifestyle factors that contribute to palpitations. Small changes can make a meaningful difference.

Respond calmly

Learning to respond to palpitations without fear. Many patients find that anxiety about symptoms makes them worse. We help you break that cycle using structured reassurance and graduated exposure techniques.

What makes this different?

Most palpitation pathways stop at testing. If the test is normal, the patient is discharged. This pathway is designed to go further — combining monitoring with a practical programme aimed at symptom improvement, not just diagnosis. This pathway combines rhythm monitoring with behavioural and physiological optimisation — an approach not commonly offered in standard palpitations care.

For Clinicians & Referrers

Rapid Rhythm Assessment Pathway

Patients may self-refer. This page is for clinicians who wish to understand the pathway or discuss patient suitability.

A structured low-risk palpitations pathway combining clinic review, stepped ECG monitoring, and a six-week behavioural and physiological optimisation programme.

Pathway focus

This pathway is designed for adults with low-risk palpitations who are otherwise well and have no high-risk features. It aims to improve access and speed for patients who might otherwise wait for routine cardiology review, while providing structured monitoring and practical support that standard pathways do not typically offer.

Inclusion criteria

  • Intermittent palpitations (awareness of heartbeat)
  • Brief episodes (seconds to minutes), settling spontaneously
  • No loss of consciousness
  • No known significant structural heart disease
  • No prior high-risk arrhythmia diagnosis
  • Generally well between episodes

Exclusion criteria

  • Syncope or pre-syncope
  • Chest pain suggestive of cardiac origin
  • Severe or unexplained breathlessness
  • Sustained tachycardia (>20–30 minutes)
  • Known structural heart disease, heart failure, or significant arrhythmia
  • Exertion-triggered symptoms
  • Strong family history of sudden cardiac death
  • Abnormal baseline ECG requiring investigation

Monitoring protocol

Stepped approach within a six-week maximum:

  1. Weeks 0–2: KardiaMobile event recording
  2. Weeks 2–4: Extended wearable ECG monitoring if no diagnostic capture (selected patients)
  3. Weeks 4–6: Further extended monitoring if clinically appropriate

Escalation thresholds

Patients are referred onward if any of the following are identified:

  • SVT or significant supraventricular tachycardia
  • Atrial fibrillation or flutter
  • Significant pauses (>3 seconds)
  • High ectopy burden (>10% of recorded beats)
  • Symptoms remaining unexplained after completion of the pathway
  • Persistent clinical concern

Referral may be to NHS or private cardiology, depending on patient preference and clinical need.

Six-week improvement programme

Runs concurrently with monitoring. Seven interventions:

  1. Structured breathing protocol
  2. Heart rhythm pattern feedback
  3. Trigger identification and mapping
  4. Structured reassurance
  5. Graded physical activity
  6. Sleep optimisation
  7. Interoceptive exposure and symptom desensitisation

This pathway aims to provide timely, structured assessment for patients with low-risk palpitations who might otherwise wait months for routine cardiology review. It is designed to complement — not replace — existing NHS and private cardiology services. Documented findings are shared with receiving clinicians to support onward care.
